Production Task

Pitch a TV Show

You have been hired as a Producer. A TV network wants a fresh show idea aimed at teenagers and young adults.

Show Name & Concept

  • •Give it a title.
  • •Describe your show in 5-6 sentences. What happens? Why would someone your age want to watch it?

Classification & Audience

Programme Type: Informative, Entertainment, or Educational? Sub-type? Explain why.
Channel: News, Sports, Entertainment, or Streaming Platform? Why does it fit there?
Target Audience: Who specifically would watch this? Be detailed (age, interests).

From the 12 professionals studied, pick the 6 most important roles you would hire FIRST.

For each role, explain:

  • •Why this role is essential for YOUR particular show.
  • •What this person would specifically do on YOUR show.

Note: Connect each role to your show's specific needs.

Walk through production using the 3 stages framework (4-5 sentences each):

Pre-Production: Research, script process, locations, equipment, costumes.
Production: Studio vs location, typical shooting day, challenges.
Post-Production: Editing style, special effects, sound design, polishing.

Write 4-5 sentences on the value of your show:

  • •Why is this show worth making?
  • •Would it inform, educate, entertain, or combine these?
  • •What positive impact might it create?
02
Media Analysis

Screen vs Paper

A Week Without Screens vs A Week Without Paper. Analyze your unique media environment.

Write an honest reflection (8-10 sentences):

  • •What is it like living with almost no screen access?
  • •What do you MISS about having screen access?
  • •What have you GAINED from limited screen access?
  • •How does screen time feel different when limited?

Answer each scenario in 2-3 sentences:

1. Exam Prep:

Textbook chapter vs YouTube video. You can only use ONE.

2. Breaking News:

Election results. Newspaper or Television?

3. Accessibility:

Grandmother doesn't read well. Pamphlet or TV?

4. Deep Understanding:

Complex topic like climate change. Newspaper series or TV?

Write 4-5 sentences:

Can television REPLACE print? Can print REPLACE television? Or do we need both?

Use characteristics and strengths of each medium.

03
Career

Where Would YOU Fit?

Make informed career choices. Understanding your own interests is valuable self-knowledge.

From the 12 professionals, pick the ONE role that interests you most. Write 8-10 sentences:

  • •Which role and why?
  • •What skills does it need?
  • •What production stage is it involved in?
  • •How has it changed in the modern era?
  • •Does this role exist outside TV?

If TV/media doesn't interest you, write 8-10 sentences:

  • •What career path ARE you interested in?
  • •How could understanding TV production still be useful?
  • •What has this module taught that applies to ANY career?
